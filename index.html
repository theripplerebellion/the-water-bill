‚ùÑÔ∏è Cooling Technology Explained

How data centers cool - and why it matters for water consumption.

Important: Heat doesn't disappear - it has to go somewhere. All cooling methods dump heat into the environment. The question is: water or air? And in what context?

Evaporative Cooling

Current Industry Standard
WATER USE
VERY HIGH - 80% evaporates
ENERGY USE
Lower (efficient)

Water pumped through cooling towers, evaporates into steam, lost forever

PROS
  • Very effective cooling
  • Lower energy than alternatives
  • Industry standard (cheap)
CONS
  • Water permanently consumed
  • Terrible in drought regions
  • Depletes local sources
  • Unsustainable at scale
WHO USES IT
Google (most facilities), Meta (many facilities), Amazon (likely, not disclosed)
VERDICT: PHASE OUT - especially in water-stressed regions

Closed-Loop Cooling

Better Alternative
WATER USE
MUCH LOWER - 90% reduction
ENERGY USE
20-30% higher (pumps, fans)

Water circulates in closed pipes, heat released to air via radiators, water recycled continuously

PROS
  • 90% less water consumption
  • Water recycled
  • Viable in water-stressed regions
  • Proven technology
CONS
  • Higher energy use
  • Higher upfront cost
  • Heat still released to air
WHO USES IT
Microsoft (new designs), Some Meta facilities, Some Google facilities
VERDICT: MANDATE in water-stressed regions

True Regenerative (Waste Heat Reuse)

Best Option (Rare)
WATER USE
MUCH LOWER - 90% reduction
ENERGY USE
Productive - heats buildings

Closed-loop + waste heat captured for district heating, turns waste into resource

PROS
  • 90% water reduction
  • Waste heat becomes useful
  • Heats homes/buildings
  • Net environmental benefit
CONS
  • Requires cold climate
  • Needs urban density
  • Needs district heating infrastructure
  • Expensive investment
WHO USES IT
Finland data centers, Sweden (Facebook), Denmark facilities
VERDICT: REQUIRE where feasible (cold, urban locations)

The Bottom Line

No perfect solution. Context matters:

  • Water-stressed regions (Arizona, California): Closed-loop mandatory
  • Fossil fuel grids: Fix energy first, then switch cooling
  • Cold + urban locations (Scandinavia): Waste heat reuse is the goal
  • Renewable grids + adequate water: Multiple options viable

The key: Match cooling to location. Stop one-size-fits-all.
